REALME BOOK LAPTOP LAUNCHING IN THE PHILIPPINES, SEPTEMBER 23, 2021

Two new Realme devices are just around the corner. In addition to the Realme GT Master Edition (our unboxing video is up), the Realme Book has also been confirmed for the local market, with the official launch date set for September 23.

Realme Philippines will bring the 11th Gen Intel Core i3 and Core i5 variants of its first laptop, and both are equipped with Iris Xe graphics, 8GB RAM, and up to 512GB of PCIe SSD storage. They will be available in Real Silver and Real Blue colors.

Local pricing will be announced during the launch, but expect aggressive price tags attached to both models. In India, where the laptop debuted last month as the Realme Book Slim, the base Core i3 variant costs ₹46,999, which translates to roughly $640 or P31,920.

Despite copying Apple's design elements, the realme Book does have solid specs for the price. First, it has a 512GB SSD drive. If you're getting a MacBook, you need to spend almost P70,000 to get the variant with that amount of fast SSD storage. The Realme Book uses an aluminium-alloy unibody design sporting a mirror logo. It’s only 15.5mm thick and weighs 1.38 kilograms. Impressively, the company’s first laptop features a 14-inch IPS display with a 2,160 x 1,440 resolution, 100% sRGB color gamut, and a tall 3:2 aspect ratio.

The Realme Book offers 400 nits of peak brightness, making it brighter than most notebooks in its class. Its thin bezels, meanwhile, bolsters its screen-to-body ratio to 90% — more than the 82% screen-to-body ratio of Apple’s MacBook Air. When it comes to multimedia features, the Book promises to deliver immersive stereo sound courtesy of two Harman Kardon speakers that also incorporate DTS audio technology. It likewise has two internal microphones that use noise-cancelling algorithms to block out background sound that might disrupt your video calls. Connectivity options on the Realme Book include Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.2, a USB 3.2 Gen 2 Type-C port, a USB-A 3.1 Gen 1 port, and a standard audio jack. The Core i5 model adds a Thunderbolt 4 port for connectivity as well as charging. Under the hood, both variants pack a 54Wh battery that is rated to deliver up to 11 hours of battery life on a single charge. It supports 65-watt fast charging, which is claimed to top up 50% of the built-in cell after just 30 minutes plugged in.

When will the AMD Ryzen 6000 series be released?

Nothing has been revealed at this early stage, so our best guess on the Ryzen 6000 release date comes from when previous generations were launched: Ryzen 1000 Series - from March 2017 Ryzen 2000 Series - from April 2018 Ryzen 3000 Series - from July 2019 Ryzen 5000 Series - from November 2020 With that in mind, it's looking likely that Ryzen 6000 CPUs will be released in early 2022. WePC is reporting on a leaked roadmap which suggests it will be unveiled sometime that year. The launch could potentially coincide with CES, which we'd expect to take place in January once again.
